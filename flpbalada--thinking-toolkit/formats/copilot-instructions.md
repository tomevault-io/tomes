# Thinking Toolkit

A curated collection of Claude Code plugins providing structured thinking
frameworks for product managers, designers, and critical thinkers.

## 📂 Plugin Map

### 🛠️ Development

- **Skill Creator** — Create new Claude Code custom skills

### 🔍 Analysis & Problem-Solving

- **Five Whys** — Find root causes of recurring problems
- **Hypothesis Tree** — Break complex questions into testable hypotheses
- **Jobs to Be Done** — Understand what users really want to achieve
- **Graph Thinking** — Map dependencies and system relationships

### 🧠 Behavior Design

- **Fogg Behavior Model** — Design for behavior change (B=MAP framework)
- **Hooked Model** — Create habit-forming product loops
- **Self-Initiated Triggers** — Build internal engagement without notifications
- **Loss Aversion** — Leverage fear of loss in retention & pricing

### 🎨 UX & Interface Psychology

- **Cognitive Load** — Reduce mental effort in complex interfaces
- **Hick's Law** — Optimize decision speed through choice reduction
- **Progressive Disclosure** — Reveal complexity gradually
- **Halo Effect** — Design powerful first impressions
- **Cognitive Fluency** — Make information feel trustworthy
- **Visual Cues & CTA** — Direct attention to conversion elements

### 🤝 Trust & Social Psychology

- **Trust Psychology** — Build credibility and reduce perceived risk
- **Social Proof** — Use collective behavior to influence decisions
- **Curiosity Gap** — Create engagement through strategic information gaps

### 📊 Strategic Planning

- **Business Model Canvas** — Design or analyze business models
- **Making Product Decisions** — Structure complex tradeoffs
- **Theme-Epic-Story** — Organize backlog hierarchically
- **User Story Fundamentals** — Write clear requirements from user perspective

### ⚙️ Workflow & Risk

- **Kanban** — Visualize and optimize work flow
- **Cognitive Biases** — Recognize systematic thinking errors
- **Status Quo Bias** — Plan for user resistance to change
- **Game Theory: Tit-for-Tat** — Navigate negotiations and repeated interactions
- **What Not to Do as PM** — Avoid common product management mistakes

### 🤖 Project Orchestrator (Agent)

1. **Discovery** → Clarify stakeholders, problem, motivation
2. **Analysis** → Root cause, hypothesis testing, relationship mapping
3. **Planning** → Strategic decisions, business design, work breakdown
4. **Psychology** → Reduce friction, build trust, drive behavior
